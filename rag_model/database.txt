=== SECTION 1: COMPLETE BLOOD COUNT (CBC) & BLOOD HEALTH ===

TOPIC: HEMOGLOBIN (Hb)
TYPE: Red Blood Cell Marker.
NORMAL RANGE: Men: 13.5-17.5 g/dL | Women: 12.0-15.5 g/dL.
CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE: Oxygen carrying capacity of blood.
LOW HEMOGLOBIN (ANEMIA) - DETAILED:
- Mild Anemia (10-12 g/dL): Often asymptomatic or mild fatigue.
- Moderate Anemia (8-10 g/dL): Palpitations, shortness of breath on exertion, pale skin.
- Severe Anemia (<8 g/dL): Chest pain, fainting, requires urgent evaluation.
CAUSES OF LOW:
- Microcytic (Small cells): Iron deficiency, Thalassemia.
- Normocytic (Normal size): Kidney disease, bone marrow suppression, chronic illness.
- Macrocytic (Large cells): B12/Folate deficiency, liver disease, alcohol use.
DIETARY PLAN FOR ANEMIA:
- Heme Iron (Best absorption): Beef, chicken liver, clams, sardines.
- Non-Heme Iron (Plant based): Spinach, lentils, kidney beans, pumpkin seeds.
- Absorption Booster: Combine iron foods with Vitamin C (oranges, bell peppers).
- Absorption Blockers: Avoid tea, coffee, and calcium supplements 1 hour before/after meals.

TOPIC: RED BLOOD CELL COUNT (RBC)
NORMAL RANGE: Men: 4.7-6.1 million/mcL | Women: 4.2-5.4 million/mcL.
HIGH RBC (ERYTHROCYTOSIS):
- Primary Cause: Polycythemia Vera (genetic).
- Secondary Causes: Low oxygen levels (COPD, Sleep Apnea), high altitude, performance-enhancing drugs (steroids).
- Hydration Note: Dehydration can cause falsely high RBC results.

TOPIC: HEMATOCRIT (Hct)
DEFINITION: The percentage of blood volume that is red blood cells.
NORMAL RANGE: Men: 40-54% | Women: 37-47%.
CORRELATION: Usually 3x the Hemoglobin value.
LOW HCT: Indicates anemia, hemodilution (too much fluid), or blood loss.
HIGH HCT: Dehydration, burns, polycythemia.

TOPIC: MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume)
DEFINITION: Average size of red blood cells.
NORMAL: 80-96 fL.
LOW MCV (<80 fL): Microcytic Anemia. Most commonly Iron Deficiency.
HIGH MCV (>96 fL): Macrocytic Anemia. Most commonly B12 or Folate deficiency, or Alcoholism.

TOPIC: RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width)
DEFINITION: Variation in red cell size.
NORMAL: 11-15%.
HIGH RDW: Indicates mixed cell sizes. Early sign of Iron Deficiency or B12 deficiency (Anisocytosis).
NORMAL RDW + LOW MCV: Thalassemia trait.
HIGH RDW + LOW MCV: Iron deficiency anemia.

TOPIC: WHITE BLOOD CELL COUNT (WBC)
FUNCTION: Immune defense.
NORMAL: 4,000 - 11,000 /mcL.
HIGH WBC (LEUKOCYTOSIS):
- Bacterial Infection: Usually correlates with high Neutrophils.
- Viral Infection: Sometimes mild elevation, but often low.
- Stress Response: Pain, surgery, trauma, or emotional stress.
- Medications: Corticosteroids (Prednisone) often increase WBC.
LOW WBC (LEUKOPENIA):
- Viral suppression (Flu, Dengue, HIV).
- Autoimmune: Lupus, Rheumatoid Arthritis.
- Medications: Chemotherapy, Methotrexate.

TOPIC: WBC DIFFERENTIAL (TYPES OF WBCs)
1. NEUTROPHILS (40-70%): Fight bacteria. High = Bacterial infection or inflammation. Low = Neutropenia (risk of infection).
2. LYMPHOCYTES (20-40%): Fight viruses. High = Viral infection (Mononucleosis, Mumps) or Lymphocytic Leukemia. Low = Steroid use, HIV, sepsis.
3. MONOCYTES (2-8%): Clean up debris. High = Chronic infection (TB), autoimmune disease.
4. EOSINOPHILS (1-4%): Fight parasites & allergies. High = Asthma, hay fever, food allergies, intestinal parasites.
5. BASOPHILS (0.5-1%): Allergic response. High = Rare allergic reactions or chronic inflammation.

TOPIC: PLATELETS (PLT)
FUNCTION: Clotting.
NORMAL: 150,000 - 450,000 /mcL.
LOW PLATELETS (THROMBOCYTOPENIA):
- Values 100k-150k: Mild, usually observation only.
- Values 50k-100k: Risk of excessive bleeding during surgery.
- Values <20k: Risk of spontaneous bleeding.
- Causes: Dengue fever, Cirrhosis (spleen sequestration), ITP, Vitamin B12 deficiency.
HIGH PLATELETS (THROMBOCYTOSIS):
- Reactive: Infection, inflammation, Iron deficiency anemia, Post-surgery.
- Essential: Bone marrow disorder.

=== SECTION 2: INFLAMMATION & RHEUMATOLOGY ===

TOPIC: ERYTHROCYTE SEDIMENTATION RATE (ESR)
WHAT IT MEASURES: How fast red cells settle. Nonspecific inflammation marker.
NORMAL: Men <15 mm/hr | Women <20 mm/hr (Age dependent).
HIGH ESR:
- Infection (Bone, heart valve).
- Autoimmune disease (Lupus, Temporal Arteritis).
- Cancer.
- Pregnancy (Normal elevation).

TOPIC: C-REACTIVE PROTEIN (CRP)
WHAT IT MEASURES: Acute phase inflammation protein from liver.
NORMAL: <1.0 mg/dL or <10 mg/L.
HIGH CRP:
- Bacterial infections (Sepsis).
- Flare-up of autoimmune disease (Crohn's, RA).
- Heart Disease Risk: High Sensitivity CRP (hs-CRP) is used to assess cardiac risk.

TOPIC: URIC ACID
NORMAL: 3.5 - 7.2 mg/dL.
HIGH URIC ACID:
- Condition: Gout (crystals form in joints, especially big toe).
- Causes: Kidney inability to clear uric acid, high fructose intake, alcohol (beer), red meat.
- Kidney Stones: Uric acid stones.
DIETARY CHANGES FOR GOUT:
- Avoid: Organ meats, shellfish, beer, high fructose corn syrup.
- Eat: Cherries (help lower levels), Vitamin C, coffee, low-fat dairy.
=== SECTION 3: DIABETES & METABOLISM ===

TOPIC: FASTING BLOOD GLUCOSE (FBS)
NORMAL: 70-99 mg/dL.
PREDIABETES (IMPAIRED): 100-125 mg/dL.
DIABETES: >126 mg/dL (on two occasions).
CRITICAL LOW (HYPOGLYCEMIA): <54 mg/dL. Requires immediate glucose.

TOPIC: POST-PRANDIAL GLUCOSE (PPBS)
TIMING: 2 hours after a meal.
NORMAL: <140 mg/dL.
PREDIABETES: 140-199 mg/dL.
DIABETES: >200 mg/dL.

TOPIC: HbA1c (GLYCATED HEMOGLOBIN)
MEANING: 3-month average sugar control.
NORMAL: <5.7%.
PREDIABETES: 5.7% - 6.4%.
DIABETES: >6.5%.
CONTROL TARGETS:
- Healthy/Young: <6.5%.
- Standard Diabetic: <7.0%.
- Elderly/Comorbidities: <8.0% (to avoid hypoglycemia risks).

TOPIC: INSULIN RESISTANCE & C-PEPTIDE
INSULIN: Hormone that lowers blood sugar.
HIGH FASTING INSULIN: Early sign of Type 2 Diabetes/Metabolic Syndrome.
C-PEPTIDE: Created when insulin is made.
- Low C-Peptide + High Sugar: Type 1 Diabetes (Pancreas failure).
- High C-Peptide + High Sugar: Type 2 Diabetes (Insulin Resistance).

=== SECTION 4: LIPID PROFILE & CARDIAC HEALTH ===

TOPIC: TOTAL CHOLESTEROL
RANGE: <200 mg/dL (Desirable).
CALCULATION: HDL + LDL + 20% of Triglycerides.

TOPIC: LDL CHOLESTEROL (Direct or Calculated)
STATUS: "Bad" cholesterol. Atherogenic.
TARGETS:
- General Population: <100 mg/dL.
- Heart Disease/Diabetes: <70 mg/dL.
- History of Heart Attack: <55 mg/dL.
LIFESTYLE TO LOWER LDL:
- Saturated Fat: Limit to <10% of calories.
- Soluble Fiber: 10-25g/day (Oatmeal, Psyllium husk).
- Sterols/Stanols: 2g/day.

TOPIC: HDL CHOLESTEROL
STATUS: "Good" cholesterol.
RISK LEVEL: <40 mg/dL (Men) | <50 mg/dL (Women).
PROTECTIVE: >60 mg/dL.
HOW TO RAISE: High-intensity interval training, heavy lifting, olive oil, purple produce (anthocyanins).

TOPIC: TRIGLYCERIDES (TG)
STATUS: Fat in blood.
NORMAL: <150 mg/dL.
HIGH (200-499): Metabolic syndrome risk.
VERY HIGH (>500): Pancreatitis risk.
MAIN CAUSES: Alcohol, sugar, refined carbs (white bread/rice), obesity, hypothyroidism.

=== SECTION 5: LIVER FUNCTION TESTS (LFT) ===

TOPIC: ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) aka SGPT
SPECIFICITY: Liver specific.
NORMAL: 7-56 U/L.
MILD ELEVATION (2x-5x): Fatty liver (NAFLD), medication effect, chronic Hep B/C.
SEVERE ELEVATION (>15x): Acute Hepatitis (Viral), Toxin ingestion (Tylenol overdose).

TOPIC: AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) aka SGOT
SPECIFICITY: Liver, Heart, Muscle, RBCs.
NORMAL: 10-40 U/L.
AST > ALT RATIO: If AST is 2x higher than ALT, strongly suggests Alcoholic Liver Disease.

TOPIC: ALKALINE PHOSPHATASE (ALP)
ORIGIN: Liver (bile ducts) and Bone.
HIGH ALP:
- With High GGT: Bile duct issue (stone, obstruction).
- With Normal GGT: Bone issue (Growth spurt in kids, Vitamin D deficiency, healing fracture).

TOPIC: BILIRUBIN (Total, Direct, Indirect)
TOTAL BILIRUBIN NORMAL: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL.
HIGH DIRECT (CONJUGATED): Post-liver blockage (Gallstone, Tumor). Causes dark urine.
HIGH INDIRECT (UNCONJUGATED): Pre-liver issue (Hemolysis/breakdown of blood cells) or Gilbert's Syndrome (harmless genetic condition).

TOPIC: ALBUMIN & PROTEIN
ALBUMIN NORMAL: 3.5-5.0 g/dL.
FUNCTION: Maintains oncotic pressure (keeps fluid in vessels).
LOW ALBUMIN:
- Liver failure (liver makes albumin).
- Nephrotic syndrome (kidney leaks albumin).
- Malnutrition.
SYMPTOM OF LOW ALBUMIN: Edema (swelling in legs/belly).

=== SECTION 6: KIDNEY FUNCTION (KFT) & ELECTROLYTES ===

TOPIC: CREATININE
SOURCE: Muscle breakdown product.
NORMAL: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL (Men) | 0.6-1.1 mg/dL (Women).
Bodybuilder Note: High muscle mass may cause naturally higher baseline (e.g., 1.4).
FALSE HIGH: Creatine supplements, heavy meat meal before test.

TOPIC: eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate)
STAGING CKD (Chronic Kidney Disease):
- Stage 1: >90 (Normal function).
- Stage 2: 60-89 (Mild loss).
- Stage 3a: 45-59 (Mild to Moderate).
- Stage 3b: 30-44 (Moderate to Severe).
- Stage 4: 15-29 (Severe).
- Stage 5: <15 (Kidney Failure/Dialysis).

TOPIC: BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen)
NORMAL: 6-20 mg/dL.
HIGH BUN: Kidney issues, Dehydration, High protein diet, GI Bleed.
BUN/CREATININE RATIO:
- >20:1 suggests Prerenal cause (Dehydration, heart failure).
- <15:1 suggests Renal cause (Kidney damage).

TOPIC: ELECTROLYTES
1. SODIUM (Na): 135-145 mEq/L.
- Low (Hyponatremia): Confusion, seizures. From diuretics, over-hydration.
2. POTASSIUM (K): 3.5-5.0 mEq/L.
- High (Hyperkalemia): Dangerous arrhythmias. Kidney failure, ACE inhibitors.
- Low (Hypokalemia): Muscle cramps, weakness. Diuretics, vomiting.
3. CHLORIDE (Cl): 96-106 mEq/L.
- Follows sodium levels to maintain acid-base balance.
=== SECTION 7: THYROID & HORMONES ===

TOPIC: TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
CONTROL: Pituitary gland.
NORMAL: 0.4 - 4.0 mIU/L.
TARGETS: Pregnancy (<2.5), Elderly (slightly higher allowed).
HIGH TSH: Hypothyroidism (Thyroid failure).
LOW TSH: Hyperthyroidism (Overactive) or pituitary failure.

TOPIC: FREE T4 (Thyroxine)
NORMAL: 0.9 - 2.3 ng/dL.
RELATIONSHIP:
- High TSH + Low T4 = Overt Hypothyroidism.
- High TSH + Normal T4 = Subclinical Hypothyroidism (Monitor).
- Low TSH + High T4 = Hyperthyroidism (Graves disease).

TOPIC: THYROID ANTIBODIES (Anti-TPO)
SIGNIFICANCE: Tests for Hashimoto's Thyroiditis.
POSITIVE: Autoimmune attack on thyroid. Common cause of Hypothyroidism.

TOPIC: CORTISOL
FUNCTION: Stress hormone.
TESTING: 8 AM (Peak) | 4 PM (Drop).
HIGH CORTISOL: Cushing's Syndrome, chronic stress, steroid use. Symptoms: Moon face, belly fat, purple stretch marks.
LOW CORTISOL: Addison's Disease (Adrenal insufficiency). Symptoms: Low BP, darkening skin, fatigue.

=== SECTION 8: VITAMINS & MINERALS ===

TOPIC: VITAMIN D (25-OH)
NORMAL: 30-100 ng/mL.
DEFICIENCY (<20 ng/mL): Bone pain, muscle weakness, depression risk, lowered immunity.
TOXICITY (>100 ng/mL): Calcium buildup in vessels, kidney stones, nausea.
DOSAGE NOTE: Supplements usually require Vitamin K2 for better absorption into bone vs arteries.

TOPIC: VITAMIN B12 (Cobalamin)
NORMAL: 200-900 pg/mL.
GRAY AREA: 200-400 pg/mL can still have neurological symptoms.
DEFICIENCY SYMPTOMS: Peripheral neuropathy (tingling), balance issues, cognitive fog, glossitis (swollen tongue).
AT RISK GROUPS: Vegans, Metformin users, Gastric bypass patients, Elderly.

TOPIC: FERRITIN
WHAT IT IS: Iron storage protein.
NORMAL: Men: 24-336 ng/mL | Women: 11-307 ng/mL.
LOW FERRITIN (<30): Iron deficiency (even if Hemoglobin is normal). First stage of anemia.
HIGH FERRITIN: Acute inflammation (acute phase reactant) or Hemochromatosis (Iron overload disease).

TOPIC: MAGNESIUM
BLOOD TEST LIMITATION: Only 1% of magnesium is in blood. Normal blood levels can mask tissue deficiency.
SYMPTOMS OF DEFICIENCY: Muscle twitches, cramps, insomnia, anxiety, palpitations.
FOOD SOURCES: Pumpkin seeds, spinach, dark chocolate, almonds.

=== SECTION 9: URINALYSIS (URINE ROUTINE) ===

TOPIC: SPECIFIC GRAVITY
NORMAL: 1.005 - 1.030.
HIGH: Dehydration or sugar in urine.
LOW: Diabetes Insipidus or excessive water drinking.

TOPIC: pH
NORMAL: 4.6 - 8.0.
ACIDIC (<5.0): High protein diet, ketoacidosis. Risk of uric acid stones.
ALKALINE (>8.0): Vegetarian diet, UTI, vomiting. Risk of phosphate stones.

TOPIC: PROTEIN (Albumin)
NORMAL: Negative or Trace.
POSITIVE (+1 to +4):
- Transient: Fever, heavy exercise, dehydration.
- Persistent: Kidney disease (Glomerulonephritis), Diabetes, Hypertension damage.

TOPIC: GLUCOSE (Sugar)
NORMAL: Negative.
POSITIVE: Blood sugar usually exceeds 180 mg/dL (Renal Threshold). Major diabetes flag.
EXCEPTION: SGLT2 inhibitor drugs (Jardiance) cause sugar in urine intentionally.

TOPIC: KETONES
NORMAL: Negative.
POSITIVE: Fat breakdown.
- Causes: Fasting/Starvation, Keto diet, Diabetic Ketoacidosis (Emergency if combined with high sugar).

TOPIC: LEUKOCYTE ESTERASE & NITRITE
NORMAL: Negative.
POSITIVE: Urinary Tract Infection (UTI).
- Leukocytes: White blood cells fighting infection.
- Nitrites: Bacteria (E. coli) converting nitrate to nitrite.

TOPIC: BLOOD (Hematuria)
NORMAL: Negative.
VISIBLE BLOOD (Gross): Bladder infection, stones, tumor.
MICROSCOPIC BLOOD (Occult): Stones, kidney inflammation, strenuous exercise.

=== SECTION 10: BLOOD PRESSURE & VITAL SIGNS ===

TOPIC: BLOOD PRESSURE CATEGORIES (AHA/ACC Guidelines)
1. NORMAL: Systolic <120 AND Diastolic <80.
2. ELEVATED: Systolic 120-129 AND Diastolic <80. (Lifestyle changes needed).
3. HYPERTENSION STAGE 1: Systolic 130-139 OR Diastolic 80-89.
4. HYPERTENSION STAGE 2: Systolic >140 OR Diastolic >90.
5. HYPERTENSIVE CRISIS: Systolic >180 and/or Diastolic >120. (Emergency).

LIFESTYLE FOR HYPERTENSION (DASH DIET):
- Sodium: Limit to <1500mg or <2300mg per day.
- Potassium: Increase intake (Bananas, potatoes, spinach).
- Weight: Loss of 1kg ~ 1 point drop in BP.
- Alcohol: Limit to 1 drink/day (women), 2 drinks/day (men).

=== SECTION 11: COMMON SYMPTOMS & TRIAGE ===

TOPIC: CHEST PAIN FLAGS
CALL EMERGENCY IF:
- Pressure/squeezing sensation ("Elephant on chest").
- Pain radiates to left arm, jaw, or back.
- Accompanied by sweating, nausea, shortness of breath.
NON-CARDIAC CAUSES: Acid reflux (burning), Costochondritis (tender to touch), Muscle strain.

TOPIC: HEADACHE FLAGS
SEE DOCTOR IF:
- "Thunderclap" headache (worst of life, sudden onset).
- Headache with fever and stiff neck (Meningitis risk).
- Headache after head injury.
- New headache in person over 50.
- Vision changes or slurred speech (Stroke risk).

TOPIC: FEVER
ADULT DEFINITION: >100.4°F (38°C).
LOW GRADE: 99°F - 100.4°F.
WHEN TO WORRY:
- Fever >103°F.
- Fever lasting >3 days.
- Fever with rash or breathing difficulty.
=== SECTION 12: CANCER MARKERS (TUMOR MARKERS) ===
NOTE: Tumor markers are not diagnostic on their own. They require imaging and biopsy for confirmation.

TOPIC: PSA (Prostate Specific Antigen)
TARGET: Prostate Cancer screening (Men).
NORMAL: <4.0 ng/mL.
GRAY ZONE: 4.0 - 10.0 ng/mL (Requires Free PSA test or MRI).
HIGH PSA:
- Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH/Enlarged Prostate).
- Prostatitis (Infection).
- Prostate Cancer.
ADVICE: Avoid bike riding or ejaculation 48 hours before testing to avoid false positives.

TOPIC: CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen)
TARGET: Colon and Rectal Cancer.
NORMAL: <2.5 ng/mL (Non-smokers) | <5.0 ng/mL (Smokers).
HIGH CEA:
- Colorectal cancer.
- Breast, lung, or pancreatic cancer.
- Non-cancerous: Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD), Heavy smoking, Pancreatitis.

TOPIC: CA-125
TARGET: Ovarian Cancer.
NORMAL: <35 U/mL.
HIGH CA-125:
- Ovarian cancer.
- Endometriosis (Common cause of false alarm).
- Fibroids, Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID).
- Pregnancy or Menstruation.

TOPIC: CA 19-9
TARGET: Pancreatic Cancer.
NORMAL: <37 U/mL.
HIGH CA 19-9:
- Pancreatic cancer.
- Gallstones or Bile duct blockage (Jaundice).
- Cystic Fibrosis.

TOPIC: AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein)
TARGET: Liver Cancer and Germ Cell Tumors.
NORMAL: <10 ng/mL.
HIGH AFP:
- Hepatocellular Carcinoma (Liver Cancer).
- Liver cirrhosis or chronic Hepatitis.
- Normal pregnancy (produced by fetus).

=== SECTION 13: CARDIAC ENZYMES & ADVANCED HEART HEALTH ===

TOPIC: TROPONIN I and T
WHAT IT IS: Gold standard for heart attack detection.
NORMAL: <0.04 ng/mL.
POSITIVE: Indicates damage to heart muscle cells (Myocardial Infarction).
TIMING: Rises within 3-4 hours of chest pain, stays high for 10-14 days.

TOPIC: CPK-MB (Creatine Phosphokinase-MB)
NORMAL: 0 - 25 IU/L.
SIGNIFICANCE: Older test for heart attack. Rises faster than Troponin but is less specific (can rise with severe muscle injury).

TOPIC: BNP (Brain Natriuretic Peptide)
TARGET: Heart Failure (CHF).
NORMAL: <100 pg/mL.
HIGH BNP (>400): Heart failure is likely. The heart is stretching and struggling to pump.
SYMPTOMS TO WATCH: Swelling in legs, shortness of breath while lying flat.

TOPIC: HOMOCYSTEINE
NORMAL: 4 - 15 micromol/L.
HIGH LEVELS:
- Increased risk of stroke and heart attack.
- Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency (these vitamins break down homocysteine).
ADVICE: Supplement with B-Complex vitamins if high.

=== SECTION 14: INFECTIOUS DISEASES (SEROLOGY) ===

TOPIC: DENGUE SEROLOGY
NS1 ANTIGEN: Positive in first 1-5 days of fever. Active infection.
IgM ANTIBODY: Positive after day 5. Recent active infection.
IgG ANTIBODY: Positive after 2-4 weeks. Indicates past infection (Immunity).
WARNING SIGNS: Drop in Platelets (<100k) + Rise in Hematocrit (Hemoconcentration).

TOPIC: TYPHOID (WIDAL TEST)
TO ANTIGEN: Active infection (Typhi O).
TH ANTIGEN: Convalescent stage (Typhi H).
INTERPRETATION: Titers >1:80 or 1:160 suggest infection.
LIMITATION: Widal often gives false positives. Typhoid Dot or Blood Culture is more accurate.

TOPIC: MALARIA PARASITE (MP)
TESTS: Blood Smear (Gold Standard) or Rapid Antigen Test.
TYPES: Plasmodium Vivax (Common, less severe) vs. Plasmodium Falciparum (Severe, cerebral malaria risk).
SYMPTOMS: Cyclical fevers, shivering chills, sweating.

TOPIC: H. PYLORI
TARGET: Stomach bacteria causing ulcers.
TESTS: Urea Breath Test, Stool Antigen, or Blood Antibody.
SYMPTOMS: Burning stomach pain, bloating, nausea.
TREATMENT: Antibiotics + Proton Pump Inhibitors (Antacids).

TOPIC: HEPATITIS PANEL
HBsAg (Surface Antigen): Positive = Active Hepatitis B infection.
Anti-HBs (Surface Antibody): Positive = Immunity (from vaccine or past infection).
Anti-HCV: Positive = Hepatitis C infection.
HAV IgM: Positive = Active Hepatitis A (food/water borne).

=== SECTION 15: AUTOIMMUNE & ARTHRITIS ===

TOPIC: RHEUMATOID FACTOR (RF)
NORMAL: <14 IU/mL.
HIGH RF: Rheumatoid Arthritis, Sjogren's syndrome, Lupus.
FALSE POSITIVES: Old age, chronic infection (TB, Hep C).

TOPIC: ANTI-CCP (Cyclic Citrullinated Peptide)
SIGNIFICANCE: More specific for Rheumatoid Arthritis than RF.
POSITIVE: High likelihood of aggressive arthritis.

TOPIC: ANA (Antinuclear Antibody)
NORMAL: Negative or Titer <1:40.
POSITIVE: Lupus (SLE), Scleroderma, Mixed Connective Tissue Disease.
NOTE: 5-10% of healthy people have a low positive ANA.

TOPIC: HLA-B27
TARGET: Ankylosing Spondylitis.
POSITIVE: Genetic marker found in people with chronic back pain/stiffness (Bamboo spine).

=== SECTION 16: PANCREAS & GASTROINTESTINAL ===

TOPIC: AMYLASE
NORMAL: 23 - 85 U/L.
HIGH: Acute Pancreatitis (Severe abdominal pain radiating to back), Mumps, Salivary gland blockage.

TOPIC: LIPASE
NORMAL: 0 - 160 U/L.
SIGNIFICANCE: More specific to pancreas than Amylase.
HIGH (>3x normal): Strong indicator of Pancreatitis or Pancreatic stones.

TOPIC: STOOL OCCULT BLOOD
NORMAL: Negative.
POSITIVE: Bleeding somewhere in the gut.
- Upper GI: Ulcers, Gastritis.
- Lower GI: Polyps, Colon Cancer, Hemorrhoids, Fissures.

=== SECTION 17: REPRODUCTIVE HORMONES ===

TOPIC: TESTOSTERONE (Total)
NORMAL MEN: 300 - 1,000 ng/dL.
LOW MEN: Low libido, erectile dysfunction, fatigue, muscle loss.
NORMAL WOMEN: 15 - 70 ng/dL.
HIGH WOMEN: PCOS (Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome), acne, excessive hair growth (hirsutism).

TOPIC: FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone)
MEN: Sperm production. High = Testicular failure.
WOMEN: Egg maturation.
- Day 3 High FSH: Low ovarian reserve (Menopause approaching).
- Low FSH: Pituitary issue or Hypothalamic amenorrhea (stress/weight loss).

TOPIC: LH (Luteinizing Hormone)
MEN: Testosterone production signal.
WOMEN: Triggers ovulation.
- LH/FSH Ratio > 2:1 is suggestive of PCOS.

TOPIC: PROLACTIN
NORMAL: <25 ng/mL.
HIGH (Hyperprolactinemia):
- Women: Irregular periods, breast milk production (galactorrhea), infertility.
- Men: Low libido, erectile dysfunction.
- Causes: Pituitary tumor (Prolactinoma), Hypothyroidism, Stress, Meds.

TOPIC: SEMEN ANALYSIS
VOLUME: >1.5 mL.
COUNT: >15 million/mL.
MOTILITY: >40% moving.
MORPHOLOGY: >4% normal shapes.
ABNORMALITIES:
- Azoospermia: Zero sperm.
- Oligospermia: Low count.
- Asthenospermia: Low movement.

=== SECTION 18: DETAILED DIETARY PROTOCOLS ===

TOPIC: DASH DIET (For High Blood Pressure)
PRINCIPLE: Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension.
EAT: Fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, low-fat dairy.
LIMIT: Red meat, sodium (salt), sweets, sugary drinks.
KEY NUTRIENTS: Potassium, Magnesium, Calcium, Fiber.

TOPIC: LOW GLYCEMIC INDEX (For Diabetes)
AVOID (High GI): White bread, white rice, corn flakes, watermelon, potatoes, sugary drinks.
EAT (Low GI): Whole oats, brown rice, sweet potato, lentils, beans, apples, berries, greek yogurt.
STRATEGY: Combine carbs with protein/fat to slow sugar absorption.

TOPIC: RENAL DIET (For Kidney Disease)
RESTRICTION 1 (Sodium): Limit salt to prevent fluid retention/BP.
RESTRICTION 2 (Potassium): If K is high, avoid bananas, oranges, potatoes, tomatoes, spinach.
RESTRICTION 3 (Phosphorus): Limit dairy, nuts, seeds, dark colas, processed meats.
PROTEIN: Moderate intake (too much stresses kidneys, too little causes muscle loss).

TOPIC: LOW PURINE DIET (For Gout/High Uric Acid)
AVOID (High Purine): Liver, kidney, heart (organ meats), anchovies, sardines, mussels, beer, gravy.
MODERATE: Beef, pork, lamb, seafood.
SAFE: Low-fat dairy, vegetables (even spinach/asparagus are safe unlike old myths), cherries, Vitamin C.

TOPIC: GLUTEN-FREE (For Celiac/Sensitivity)
AVOID: Wheat, Barley, Rye. (Bread, pasta, beer, soy sauce, pastries).
SAFE GRAINS: Rice, Quinoa, Corn, Buckwheat, Millet, Oats (certified GF).
HIDDEN SOURCES: Salad dressings, soups (as thickener), processed meats.

=== SECTION 19: VITAMIN & DRUG INTERACTIONS ===

TOPIC: GRAPEFRUIT JUICE WARNING
INTERACTION: Blocks CYP3A4 enzyme in liver.
RISK: Increases concentration of drugs to toxic levels.
DRUGS AFFECTED: Statins (Atorvastatin), Calcium Channel Blockers, Anti-anxiety meds.

TOPIC: VITAMIN K & BLOOD THINNERS
DRUG: Warfarin (Coumadin).
INTERACTION: Vitamin K helps blood clot, opposing the drug.
ADVICE: Maintain consistent intake of greens (spinach/kale). Do not suddenly stop or start eating huge amounts.

TOPIC: CALCIUM & ANTIBIOTICS
INTERACTION: Calcium binds to Tetracycline/Ciprofloxacin antibiotics in the stomach.
RESULT: Drug is not absorbed and doesn't work.
ADVICE: Separate dairy/supplements and antibiotics by 2 hours.

TOPIC: IRON ABSORPTION
BOOSTERS: Vitamin C (Orange juice).
BLOCKERS: Tannins (Tea/Coffee), Calcium (Milk/Cheese), Antacids.
ADVICE: Take iron on empty stomach or with Vitamin C, 2 hours away from tea/milk.

TOPIC: BIOTIN (Vitamin B7) INTERFERENCE
ISSUE: High dose Biotin (for hair/nails) ruins lab tests.
AFFECTED TESTS: Thyroid (TSH looks low, T4 looks high - mimics Graves disease), Troponin (Heart attack test).
ADVICE: Stop Biotin supplements 3-5 days before blood work.

=== SECTION 20: PEDIATRIC REFERENCE RANGES (Brief) ===

TOPIC: PEDIATRIC HEMOGLOBIN
Newborn: 14-24 g/dL (Naturally high).
Infant (1-6 mo): 9.5-14 g/dL (Physiological anemia is normal).
Child (1-6 yr): 9.5-14 g/dL.
Adolescent: Approaches adult levels.

TOPIC: PEDIATRIC HEART RATE
Newborn: 100-160 bpm.
Toddler: 90-150 bpm.
Preschool: 80-140 bpm.
School Age: 70-120 bpm.
Adolescent: 60-100 bpm.

TOPIC: PEDIATRIC JAUNDICE
Physiological: Starts Day 2-3, peaks Day 5, resolves by Day 14.
Pathological: Starts within 24 hours. Requires phototherapy.
